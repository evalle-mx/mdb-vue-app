PAID, but unable to connect

I understand that you are having connectivity issues after removing a suspension on your organization; consider that when your account is SUSPENDED, this means that has at least one failed payment more than 30 days past due, and once this suspension takes effect, backups are automatically stopped (not deleted). Also, the network access list and security configuration could be deleted/removed in this period. In order to remove this SUSPENDED state, the Owner must pay any outstanding bills to resume service, and after the pending invoices have been covered, your service should be resumed.

However, if after this suspended state is removed (being healthy or in WARNING state) and your client or application is unable to connect, you will need to verify that the client/application IP address is listed in your Network IP Access List, and/or has an Active Peering Connection or Private Endpoint.

Note that since there is no feature to restore the Access list, it would be required to Manually Add the IP Address to Your IP Access List or Configure your Peering Connection, Note that if this is urgent or you have no idea what the IP's were, add TEMPORARY access From anywhere to un-restrict Ips.

(Later, you could find the previous entries added by filtering the Project Activity Feed by events related to Network Permission Entry).
